Cub Scouts and Boy Scouts alike will find this hands-on experience something they will talk about and remember for years to come.  A few general notes for both Cub Scouts and Boy Scouts particpating in one of the Scoutmaster Bucky Horsemanship events is that

1. Scouts should dress for the weather. Long pants and boots with a heel or solid soled shoes are REQUIRED. All riders must wear an equestrian helmet, which is provided by the stable. Please understand that this program is not designed for riders with special needs.

2. A Permission Slip needs to be signed by a parent/guardian and brought on the day of the ride in order to fully participate. You can download the Permission Slip here .

3. Scouts WILL be working directly with horses both on the ground as well mounted and riding them.  Scouts will spend a good portion of the class in direct contact with the horses.

Cub Scouts - Come ready to learn and have fun as you work towards getting your Horsemanship Belt Loop

Boy Scouts - Participating Boy Scouts should come having reviewed the requirements listed in the Horsemanship Merit Badge Pamphlet.   Preparation ahead of time will ensure participating Scouts get the most out of this opportunity.

You can also review the requirements by going to the Merit Badges Page on www.ScoutmasterBucky.com and choose the Horsemanship Merit Badge.  Download the Scoutmaster Bucky Horsemanship Merit Badge Workbook and also review the Class Preparation Page for this class, PRIOR to the day of the class.  The Class Preparation Page will help you prepare for the class as well share with you the Required Materials you will need for this class.
