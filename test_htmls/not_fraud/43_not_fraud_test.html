Food, fun, and fashion all for a good cause are what makes up the Nellie's Catwalk for Kids (NC4K) fashion show. Join Nellie along with 300 local models as they take the stage to stand up against childhood cancer on Friday August 5th at Ohio Dominican University. The fashion show is being dedicated to three children, Faith Pruden, Sam Bish, and Violet Baier, that lost their battle to childhood cancer this past year.

The fashion show is made up of five entertaining scenes that will go back in time, starting with the roaring twenties, all the way up to today's trends.

The pre-party starts outside Ohio Dominican University at 6:00PM. The pre-party is for all fashion show guests and will include free food from local restaurants, a live local band, photobooth, silent auction, cash bar, and the Nellie's shop filled with NC4K merchandise, trendy accessories, and booths of the presenting sponsors. The fashion show portion will begin at 7:00PM with emcees that are the Nellie's News anchors, which are directily touched by childhood cancer. Tickets to attend are $20.00 in advance and $25.00 at the door.

The NC4K fashion show is presented by Olentangy Pediatrics, Key Bank, Round 2, Miche Bag, and Knights of Columbus.

To purchase tickets, make a donation, or to find out more information log onto www.catwalk4kids.com and to find Nellie's fan page on Facebook search for Nellie's Catwalk for Kids.

Background Information:

Nellie's Catwalk for Kids (NC4K) was started back in 2006 by Nellie Krumlauf when she was 16 years old in the efforts of bringing childhood cancer to the forefront. The organization started off as just a summer fashion show wanting to get local kids and teens involved in helping make a magical difference. NC4K has now evolved to a 501 C3 non-profit that has four main community events a year including: a dance marathon, 5K run, golf outing, and the fashion show. In addition to community events NC4K has over 15 high school Nellie's Clubs all ran by upperclassmen. The first Nellie's Club was started back in 2009 by Jessica Soroky at Pickerington North High School; she is now the manager of all of the clubs and helping NC4K expand to other high schools. NC4K's mission is to actively help kids and teens and their families that are touched by cancer. The money raised from all of our events help us work in conjunction with Nationwide Children's Hospital Oncology Unit by supporting the families in every way possible. Funds raise also help us raise more awareness for pediatric cancer and grow the organization as a whole. NC4K's main goals are to inform communities of how they can help pediatric cancer families, to raise more funds for families with a child battling cancer, to empower kids, teens and young adults to make a difference, to eventually expand outside of Ohio to help other families, and the ultimate goal of helping find a cure!

Ways to get involved:

NC4K has many easy and fun ways to give back. The most popular way is to become a summer fashion show model, along with starting a Nellie's Club at a high school to raise funds and awareness for NC4K. Other involvement opportunities include: volunteering at events, financial support, making fleece blankets for patients, collecting pop tabs for the hospital, hosting a fundraiser to benefit NC4K, and simply spreading the word.
